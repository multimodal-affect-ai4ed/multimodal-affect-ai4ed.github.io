Overview

[cite_start]As artificial intelligence becomes increasingly embedded in educational contexts, the ability of AI systems to perceive, interpret, and respond to learners' affect has shifted from a niche research interest to a central necessity[cite: 11]. [cite_start]While traditional research has prioritized "cold" cognitive processes, affect represents the "hot" functional dimension that is intrinsically intertwined with learning outcomes[cite: 18].

[cite_start]The Multimodal Affective Analytics in AI (MAAAI) workshop focuses on (1) AI-driven approaches in detecting and interpreting affective states through multimodal data streams and (2) the design of affect-aware AI systems that support emotional regulation and learner well-being[cite: 13]. [cite_start]By fostering interdisciplinary dialogue among researchers, designers, and practitioners, the workshop aims to advance ethically responsible affect-aware AI systems for education[cite: 14].

Bio

[cite_start]Roger Azevedo is a professor in the School of Modeling Simulation and Training in University of Central Florida[cite: 47]. [cite_start]He is also an affiliated faculty in the Department of Computer Science and the Department of Internal Medicine at the University of Central Florida and is the lead scientist for the Learning Sciences Faculty Cluster Initiative[cite: 48]. [cite_start]His main research area includes examining the role of cognitive, metacognitive, affective, and motivational self-regulatory processes during learning with advanced learning technologies (e.g., intelligent tutoring systems, hypermedia, multimedia, simulations, serious games, immersive virtual learning environments)[cite: 49].

Call for Participation

[cite_start]We invite researchers, designers, and practitioners from the AIED, EDM, and Learning@Scale communities to participate[cite: 34]. [cite_start]Attendees are encouraged to submit 4-8 page position papers detailing recent empirical research or theoretical frameworks regarding multimodal affective analytics in education[cite: 99].

[cite_start]The workshop's mission is structured around two central pillars[cite: 26]:

  • Pillar 1: Detection and Interpretation of Affect in Learning Settings. [cite_start]Leveraging advanced AI techniques to decode complex affective states in real-time[cite: 27].
  • Pillar 2: Affect-Aware Design. [cite_start]Moving beyond detection to the creation of AI interventions that actively scaffold emotional regulation and promote learner well-being[cite: 29].

[cite_start]

Submission Format: 4-8 page position papers[cite: 99].

[cite_start]

Review Process: These submissions will undergo a peer-review process by the organizing committee to ensure relevance and academic rigor[cite: 100].

[cite_start]

Dissemination: To ensure a lasting impact, all accepted position papers will be published in a dedicated digital repository, such as the CEUR Workshop Proceedings (CEUR-WS)[cite: 126].
